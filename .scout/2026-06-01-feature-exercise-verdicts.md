## Scout's Top 5 Opinions

### 1. Fix the diagnosis before claiming the moat — two P0 bugs are hiding your differentiator

The headline claim ("root-cause diagnosis no competitor does") is half-built in production. Error tags are authored-positional (first tag the content author chose), not observed from the learner's actual answer. `computeProductionGap` has zero runtime callers and has never fired in any live session. These two bugs mean:
- The fingerprint records what the sentence *could* test, not what the learner *did* wrong.
- The production-bias scheduler arm never activates.
- Diagnosis rules 2, 3, and 4 partially or fully inherit the authored-tag flaw.

Fix: (a) narrow each sentence to exactly one `errorTagsDetectable` tag — the one the exercise type CAN detect, not all possible errors in the sentence; (b) wire `computeProductionGap` into `recordResult`. These are code fixes, not content work. Until done, every "diagnosis" claim in marketing copy is an overclaim. After they are done, NorskCoach's diagnostic is genuinely unmatched in the Norwegian-app space.

### 2. Speaking must be in the session loop — not an opt-in silo

The north star is "speaking confidence." Speaking lives on three separate surfaces (/roleplay, /shadow, /listen) that a default-path learner never discovers. The session "Snakk" block is typed translation and word-order — Norwegian production, but not speaking. Duolingo integrates speaking exercises directly in the lesson flow (speak this sentence) and reports that learners who use speaking exercises retain 2x as long. NorskCoach has the infrastructure (speech recognition, MP3 audio) and the surfaces (/listen is confirmed real speaking with fingerprint feedback). The fix is integration, not new building: bring one /listen-style item INTO the Snakk block (one spoken response per session, scored and logged to the fingerprint). That single change makes the north star reachable from day one, not as a menu item.

### 3. Cloze-passage is the highest-leverage investment — build 10 passages per level before anything else

Cloze at discourse level is where NorskCoach is genuinely differentiated from every Norwegian competitor. Duolingo, Babbel, Lingu, Skapago — none have multi-sentence cloze with per-gap fingerprinting. Clozemaster proved the type at scale (100k+ sentences, strong retention results). NorskCoach has the architecture (per-gap SRS, per-gap error logging). The gap is content: 5 passages at A1/A2, 0 at B1/B2. A B1 learner who should benefit most from discourse-level practice sees nothing. Build 10 passages per level (40 total), each covering a weekly-sprint concept cluster. That is the content investment that no competitor can replicate quickly, because it requires the concept-tagged multi-sentence structure that only NorskCoach's corpus pipeline produces.

### 4. Speed-round and translation-to-english are category errors in a production-north-star product

Speed-round is a recognition warm-up mechanic — it produces no Norwegian output, has a wrong error tag (`spelling` on an English-answer task), and has 0 corpus at B2. Translation-to-english is comprehension-checking — correct, but at B2 it is a regression (advanced learners need more production, not more recognition). Both types currently count toward or fill slots in pools that should be production-dominant. The fix is reclassification, not deletion: speed-round → review-only warm-up (never in remediation/new-material pool); translation-to-english → explicit comprehension-check type, flagged as recognition, excluded from production guarantee. Neither type earns a place in the core remediation or new-material pools of a product whose moat is production coaching.

### 5. Roleplay is a demo, not a feature — 3 scenarios cannot build speaking confidence

The evidence from Pimsleur (50-year methodology), Speechling (human coaching at scale), and ACTFL oral proficiency research is consistent: speaking confidence requires volume (dozens of scenarios across topics and registers, not 3). NorskCoach's roleplay surface is architecturally correct — speech recognition, feedback, fingerprint badge — but 3 A1-level scenarios mean a learner exhausts it on day one and never returns. The minimum for a meaningful roleplay surface is 15–20 scenarios across A1-B2, with fingerprint-biased scenario selection (weak on modal verbs → queue the "asking permission" scenario). This is a content problem, not an engineering problem. The content pipeline (AI generation + linguist gate) can produce scenario scripts faster than any other exercise type because they don't require CEFR-tagged sentence corpora — they require dialogue templates. Roleplay at depth is the feature that would earn a "best speaking tool for Norwegian" claim that no current competitor can match.
